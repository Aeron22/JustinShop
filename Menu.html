Americano

Price: $1.2

Ingredients:
Espresso shots and Hot water

An Americano is a type of coffee made by diluting espresso with hot water. It is believed to have originated during World War II when American soldiers in Italy would add hot water to their espresso shots to make it taste more like the coffee they were used to back home.

Black Coffee

Price: $1

Ingredients:
Coffee beans and Hot water

Black coffee is a type of coffee that is served without any additives such as milk, cream, or sugar. It is made by brewing coffee beans with hot water and served as-is. Black coffee has a strong and bitter taste due to the absence of any sweeteners or dairy products.

Cappuccino

Price: $1.5

Ingredients:
Espresso, Milk and Milk foam

A cappuccino is an espresso-based coffee drink that is traditionally made with equal parts of espresso, steamed milk, and milk froth. The espresso is typically poured into a cup, followed by the steamed milk, and then topped with the frothed milk. The result is a creamy and smooth coffee beverage with a signature foam layer on top.

Espresso

Ingredients:
Ground coffee (French or other dark roast), Hot water and Lemon twists (optional)

Espresso coffee is a type of coffee that is made by forcing hot water through finely ground coffee beans at high pressure. Espresso has a rich, bold flavor and a creamy texture, with a layer of crema on top.

Iced Latte

Ingredients:
Espresso, Sweetener (optional), Flavoring syrup (optional) and Milk

An iced latte is a coffee-based beverage that is served cold. It is made by combining espresso with cold milk and ice. The milk is usually frothed or steamed to create a smooth and creamy texture, and the drink is often sweetened with a flavored syrup or sugar.

A macchiato is a type of coffee beverage typically made with espresso and a small amount of milk. The term "macchiato" means "stained" or "spotted" in Italian, referring to the small amount of milk that is added to the espresso.
